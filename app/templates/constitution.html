The Living Document

The Indian Constitution is the longest and most detailed constitution in the world. It serves as a dynamic framework, evolving through amendments to meet the needs of a modern democratic India.

Featured Articles

Article 14

Equality Before Law

The state shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.

Key Principles:
  • Prohibits discrimination
  • Rule of Law foundation
  • Equal legal protection
Article 19

Freedom of Speech

Ensures the right to freedom of speech and expression, peaceful assembly, association, and movement across India.

Key Liberties:
  • Six fundamental freedoms
  • Democratic pillar
  • Reasonable restrictions
Article 21

Life and Liberty

Guarantees that no person shall be deprived of their life or personal liberty except according to legal procedures.

Core Protections:
  • Right to dignity
  • Privacy & Education
  • Broad judicial scope
Article 32

Constitutional Remedies

The right to approach the Supreme Court directly for the enforcement of Fundamental Rights.

Legal Power:
  • Writ jurisdiction
  • Guardian of rights
  • Direct SC access
Article 51-A

Fundamental Duties

Outlines the duties of citizens towards the nation, including promoting harmony and protecting sovereignty.

Responsibilities:
  • 11 core duties
  • Civic consciousness
  • Moral obligations
Article 226

High Court Power

Empowers High Courts to issue writs for the enforcement of rights and other legal purposes.

Judicial Writ:
  • Wide jurisdiction
  • Local legal aid
  • Speedy remedies
