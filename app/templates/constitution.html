Did You Know?

The Indian Constitution is the longest and most detailed constitution in the world, containing 395 articles divided into 22 parts and 12 schedules.

Featured Articles

Article 14

Equality Before Law

The state shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.

Key Points:

• Applies to all persons, not just citizens

• Prohibits arbitrary discrimination

• Ensures equal treatment under law

Article 19

Freedom of Expression

All citizens have the right to freedom of speech and expression, assembly, association, movement, and profession.

Key Points:

• Six freedoms for all citizens

• Subject to reasonable restrictions

• Foundation of democracy

Article 21

Life and Liberty

No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to the procedure established by law.

Key Points:

• Broadest interpretation by courts

• Includes right to education, health

• Protection of human dignity

Article 32

Constitutional Remedies

The right to move the Supreme Court for the enforcement of Fundamental Rights is itself a Fundamental Right.

Key Points:

• Access to courts for justice

• Supreme Court has special powers

• Protector of Fundamental Rights

Article 51-A

Fundamental Duties

Citizens have duties like defending the country, promoting harmony, protecting public property, and developing scientific temper.

Key Points:

• 11 duties listed for citizens

• Balance between rights and duties

• Moral and legal obligations

Article 226

High Court Jurisdiction

High Courts have power to issue writs (Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Prohibition, etc.) for enforcement of rights.

Key Points:

• Tool for judicial review

• Protection against arbitrary action

• Broader scope than Article 32
